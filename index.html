SANUM2020 News

It is with great regret that we must inform you that the SANUM2020 conference scheduled for March 25-27, 2020 is hereby cancelled.

This follows an announcement from the Rector of Stellenbosch University that all conferences to be held on SU campuses must be cancelled until further notice as a precautionary measure in response to the COVID-19 pandemic.

The SANUM2020 organising committee will have an emergency meeting on Monday 16 March to discuss possible options (such as postponing the conference until later in the year, or having a special session in the SAMS conference in November) and will announce any updates via email and on the conference website as soon as possible.

We apologise for this unfortunate news, and send our best wishes to all delegates and their families in this difficult time.

What is SANUM?

South African Numerical and Applied Mathematics (SANUM) is a special interest group for South African and international academics in the areas of applied mathematics, numerical analysis, and scientific computing, and a yearly (typically three-day) symposium with presentations and posters on these and related topics.

The symposium currently rotates between three host institutions: Stellenbosch University, the University of Pretoria, and the University of the Witwatersrand. It serves as the annual symposium for the Numerical and Applied Mathematics research theme of the NRF Centre of Excellence in Mathematical and Statistical Sciences (CoE MaSS).

SANUM acknowledges sponsorship from the DSI-NRF Centre of Excellence in Mathematical and Statistical Sciences
